## Corviknight

###attack

* 108.63 attack gives a sand attack breakpoint vs rank #1 shadow alolan sandslash to flip the 0s. 110.19 attack guarantees the breakpoint vs max defense shadow alolan sandslash

* 108.79 attack lets you beat the rank #1 shadow gyarados in the 2s as long as you land a payback (the attack benchmark does one more damage with payback, otherwise gyarados wins on 1hp). this is not necessary for default IV shadow gyarados

* 109.44 attack gives a sand attack breakpoint vs rank #1 non-shadow blastoise. 109.76 attack covers most blastoise IV spreads (all but the 50 iv spreads with 143.11+ defense). 112.03 attack guaratnees the breakpoint vs max defense blastoise. the breakpoint lets you win the 2s, as well as the 0s if you have 143+ hp and the rollout bulkpoint which requires 127.73 def vs default blastoise IVs (125.46 defense for rank #1 blastoise)

* 109.55 attack gives a sand attack breakpoint vs rank #1 cradily before the first rock tomb. the matchups are all over the place with air cutter boosts and rock romb debuffs and air cutter baits vs paybacks and what gets shielded vs what lands, but the breakpoint lets you flip the 2-1s if they shield an air cutter (or if you get the boost), the 1s if you get an immediate boost, or the 0s with a boost on the first or second air cutter

* 109.94 attack gives a sand attack breakpoint vs rank #1 diggersby. 110.59 attack guarantees the breakpoint vs max defense diggersby. this flips the 1s, the 1-0s, the 2-1s, and you usually flip the 2s with 143+ hp as well. (i say "usually" because this could be iv dependent if your defense is so low that fire punch does 48 damage, but even then, you might do more damage with your charged moves to win anyway. i would sim the matchup yourself to be sure, but the breakpoint with 143+ hp looks like a pretty good catch-all)

* 111.47 attack gives a sand attack breakpoint on rank #1 shadow drapion. shadow drapion has an azumarill bulkpoint around 138+ defense and is a common stat goal for shadow drapion, so the rank #1 (139 defense) seems like a good IV spread to compare with. 111.25 attack gets the breakpoint against 138.74 defense shadow drapion, but any less attack makes the breakpoint very inconsistent because you will never get the breakpoint on shadow drapion with more than 138.38 defense. the breakpoint flips the 1-2s

* 113.39 attack (max attack) gives a sand attack breakpoint on most forretress before the first rock tomb. 111.25 attack gets a breakpoint against the rank #1, but the rank #1 is a little skewed because the iv spread is 0/9/15. the rank 2 forretress (1/15/15) requires max attack corviknight to get the breakpoint and feels more reasonable. you need as much as 112.03 attack just for default iv forretress (5/15/13) because that has more defense than the rank #1. there is also a bug bite bulkpoint: you need 134.04 defense to cover the default iv spread before the first sand tomb, or 132.61 defense to cover the rank 1 before the first sand tomb

* 113.39 attack gives a sand attack breakpoint on rank #1 empoleon, which flips the 0s



### defense & hp:

* 124.82 defense with 145+ hp is needed to beat rank #1 and default IV wigglytuff in the 0s. 124.82 defense with 147+ hp is needed against wigglytuff focused on defense (simmed against the 3/15/1). more defense with less hp (or vice versa) will also do the trick. if you boost on the first air cutter, you win the 0s regardless. i still cannot believe there is a universe where corviknight loses to wigglytuff

* 125.31 defense with 141+ hp is needed to beat max defense good friend trade regidrago (1/15/10, the rank #1 out of good friend trade IV spreads) in the 0s. the 12/15/11 regidrago (chosen due to having the highest attack stat out of the research IV floor spreads with max defense) also seems like a good IV spread to sim against, and requires 125.93 defense with 143+ hp. in both cases you can drop one of defense or hp and compensate with more of the other stat, if needed, but i suggest doing the sims with your specific iv spread(s) to check

* 126.72 defense with 145+ hp is needed to beat aura sphere togekiss in the 0s. you can drop one of defense or hp and compensate with more of the other stat, if needed, but i suggest doing the sims with your specific iv spread(s) to check

* 127.36 defense with 149+ hp lets you beat default iv shadow dragonite (4/13/14) in the 2s. the defense benchmark is to reduce charged move damage, so you can have less of one stat with more of the other

* 127.59 defense gives a bulkpoint vs lapras with 105 attack or lower which flips the 0s and 2s. you can flip the 1s with the bulkpoint and 149+ hp

* 129.07 defense gets a bulkpoint vs slight attack weight shadow scizor. having the bulkpoint flips the 0-1s if you also have 145+ hp

* 129.69 defense with 147+ hp lets you beat default iv greninja (5/12/12) in the 2s. 130.69 defense gives a bulkpoint vs default iv water shuriken greninja, but does not flip any matchups

* 130.23 defense gives a mud shot bulkpoint vs default IV (4/15/10) shadow quagsire that flips the 0s. you can still win this without the breakpoint if you have enough hp to survive the fast moves + aqua tail + stone edge (or with an attack weight that lets you win cmp tie). the bulkpoint also flips the 1s and 2s

* 131.16 defense with 151+ hp flips the 0s vs default IV turtonator (5/15/15), but this can be inconsistent since the defense goal is to reduce brutal swing damage and turtonator IVs are difficult to control. no IV spread can flip the 0s vs the 10/14/13 turtonator (highest rank research IV floor turtonator)

* 131.16 defense with 150+ hp lets you beat default IV shadow dragonair (4/15/14) in the 1-2s. 129.69 defense with 150+ hp works vs the rank #1 shadow dragonair, but the default seems like a better comparison after accounting for the rarity of shadow dratini

* 132.10 defense with 148+ hp is necessary to win against bastiodon in the 1s. less defense with more hp works too. less hp with more defense might work too, but this is less likely. fewer IV spreads can reduce flamethrower damage further without giving too much hp in the process

* 132.19 defense gives a bulkpoint vs the rank #1 air slash mandibuzz which flips the 0s and 1s (or you can get lucky with air cutter boosts to flip the 1s regardless). it also lets you win the 1-2s depending on air cutter boosts. this can be inconsistent because of how rare vullaby is (outside of the occasional event), so IVs are likely more varied. this is bad for corviknight, since max defense corviknight loses the bulkpoint against a 7/15/15 mandibuzz

* 132.61 defense gives a bulkpoint against slight attack weighted gastrodon (and the rank #1 by extension), which flips the 2s if neither of you bait. with baits (and air cutter boosts), there are so many potential outcomes. corviknight is more likely to win in general, but the bulkpoint could help with consistency

* 133.40 defense gives a bulkpoint vs most galarian corsola (99.55 and less attack), which catches iv spreads like 5/15/15. the bulkpoint is impossible to get against the common egg iv galarian corsola (10/15/14). i imagine most people would keep trading until they get a very high rank corsola to build, so 99.55 attack seems like a safe benchmark. 130.69 defense with 150hp lets you land another payback against very high rank corsola in the 2s. doesnt flip the matchup (and is easily lost if corsola has more attack), but the difference between losing to a corsola with 97 hp and a corsola with 18 hp could be very impactful if you happen to get it

* 134.15 defense gives a bulkpoint vs the rank 1 shadow alolan sandslash, but this seems inconsistent. max defense corviknight loses the breakpoint against default IV sandslash (4/13/15), but it does flip the 0s if you get it

* 134.81 defense gives a bulkpoint against most bulky shadow dusclops, but you win all evens anyway. if you happen to face the rank #1 shadow dusclops, attack weighted corviknight can lose the 1s but the bulkpoint isnt necessary to win, as long as you have 130.16+ defense with 148+ hp. unnecessary vs most shadow dusclops iv spreads, but could be useful in those rare instances

* 135.46 defense (max defense) picks up a bulkpoint vs default iv shadow sableye (default used here since shadow sableye tend to be attack weighted) which flips the 2s if you bait twice, flips the 1 without baiting (but the rank #1 corviknight can reach a payback to win the 1s despite not having the bulkpoint). 132.75 defense gets the bulkpoint vs rank #1 shadow sableye

### the mirror match

* in the 0s, cmp tie usually determines the winner. it is possible to out-bulk most attack-weighted corviknights, but doing so is very difficult. the rank #2 (0/14/13) covers most corviknight with the combination of enough defense to reduce payback damage and enough hp to survive two paybacks and is the only IV spread that would be able to pull it off consistently (against corviknight below 112.03 attack). corviknight with 112.03+ attack win cmp tie against almost every other IV spread and do enough damage to KO after two paybacks

* in the 1s, cmp tie determines the winner

* in the 2s, most matchups will be determined by payback cmp tie. however, running a big attack weight risks dropping too much bulk and fainting to air cutter (and winning cmp no longer matters since their own air cutter is not enough to KO many opposing corviknights in that situation). high stat product corviknight almost always lose because they lose cmp tie. an IV spread that strikes the right balance of winning cmp ties without giving up too much bulk would work best. i would personally aim for 125.3+ defense and 146+ hp with as much attack as possible

### specific IV suggestions

* i think most corviknights should have at least 127.59 defense for the lapras bulkpoint, unless you have a very specific stat goal in mind that does not require the bulkpoint

* after that, an attack-weighted corviknight should aim for 111.47+ attack to reach the shadow drapion breakpoint. i would consider any corviknight with 111.47+ attack and 127.59+ defense a good option for an attack weight (and you can put those numbers into pvpivs to see what IV spreads reach those stats)

* a bulk-weighted corviknight should aim for 132.10 defense with 149 hp at minimum. the highest attack a corviknight with those stats can have is 106.67 attack. this does not reach any significant breakpoints and mainly would serve to help in cmp ties. and the most relevant one is the mirror match. i would prefer investing completely in bulk and playing under the assumption that i lose cmp tie (just knowing that you would lose cmp tie can be useful because it gives you more information than your opponent, and you can play around the fact that you will probably lose cmp tie). still, being able to win cmp is important in many situations too, so there is some merit to investing in attack after reaching the bulk benchmarks
